Ford Mustang: America's Muscle Car

Cars · Muscle Car

60 years in production. Through oil crises, emissions regulations, and changing tastes. The Mustang endures.

The Birth: 1964

Ford wanted a sporty, affordable car for young buyers. They nailed it. The Mustang sold over a million units in its first two years.

The Muscle Era: 1967-1973

Big engines. V8s. Shelby GT500s. This is the Mustang everyone remembers. Raw power, minimal refinement, maximum attitude.

The Dark Years: 1974-1993

The Mustang II was a Pinto in a costume. The Fox Body saved it — affordable, moddable, and RWD. Tuners loved it.

The Modern Era

2005 brought retro styling. 2015 brought independent rear suspension. The Mustang became a real sports car while keeping its identity.

The Electric Mustang Controversy

The Mach-E is an SUV. Calling it a Mustang angered purists. But it's selling well. The nameplate is evolving — again.

Final Thought

The Mustang survived because Ford adapted without losing its soul. V8, RWD, affordable performance. That formula still works.
