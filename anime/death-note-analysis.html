Death Note — Where It Peaked and Why

Anime · Psychological Thriller

Let's be honest: Death Note's first half is a masterpiece. The second half? Frustrating. But understanding where it peaked — and why it fell — makes it a better experience.

The First Half: Chess With Lives

Light versus L. Every episode is a mind game. The tension is unbearable. The strategies are brilliant. This is peak Death Note — two geniuses locked in battle, each move calculated, each mistake fatal.

Light Yagami: The Protagonist You Root Against

Light isn't a hero. He's a narcissist with a god complex. But he's compelling because the show never pretends otherwise. Watching his descent is the entire point.

L: The Eccentric Genius

L is weird, brilliant, and morally ambiguous. His methods are questionable. His deductions are flawless. The cat-and-mouse dynamic between him and Light is what makes the show work.

The Decline After Episode 25

When L exits, the show loses its center. The new characters don't have the same weight. The stakes feel manufactured. It's not bad — it's just a shadow of what came before.

The Ending: Poetic Justice

Despite the middle dragging, the finale delivers. Light's downfall is brutal, pathetic, and earned. He dies alone, screaming, exactly as he deserved.

Final Thought

Death Note is worth watching for the first half alone. The second half is flawed, but the ending redeems it. Just know what you're getting into.
