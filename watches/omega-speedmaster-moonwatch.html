The only watch worn on the moon. That alone makes it legendary. But there's more to it.

The NASA Connection

In 1965, NASA tested every chronograph they could find. The Speedmaster survived. It's been flight-qualified ever since.

Apollo 11 and Buzz Aldrin

July 20, 1969. Neil Armstrong left his Speedmaster in the lunar module. Buzz Aldrin wore his on the moon. History was made.

Why It's Still Relevant

Manual-wind movement. Hesalite crystal. No date complication. It's the same watch astronauts wore. That's rare in modern watchmaking.

The Community

Speedmaster owners are obsessive. They track serial numbers, production years, dial variations. It's a rabbit hole with no bottom.

Final Thought

The Speedmaster isn't just a watch. It's a piece of space exploration history you can wear. That's irreplaceable.
