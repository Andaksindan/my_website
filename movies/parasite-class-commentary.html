Parasite and Class Warfare

Movies · Thriller

Bong Joon-ho's masterpiece isn't subtle about class. It's operatic, darkly comedic, and ultimately tragic. And it deserved every award it won.

The Con That Feels Too Real

The Kim family infiltrates the Park household one by one. It's clever, funny, and increasingly uncomfortable as you realize how desperate they are.

The Basement Reveal

Halfway through, the film shifts. What seemed like a dark comedy becomes a horror movie. The revelation of what's hidden below changes everything.

No Heroes, Only Victims

The Kims aren't heroes. The Parks aren't villains. They're all trapped in a system that pits them against each other while the real problem goes unaddressed.

The Final Act: Chaos and Consequence

The birthday party descends into violence. Not because anyone is evil — but because desperation, humiliation, and resentment explode at once.

The Ending: A Dream Deferred

Ki-woo's plan to buy the house and free his father is heartbreaking. It's mathematically impossible. And that's the point.

Final Thought

Parasite works because it doesn't preach. It shows. And what it shows is a world where upward mobility is a fantasy sold to keep people hoping.
